Kung Pao Chicken

This is a scrumptious looking asian dish served with a side of rice. It is meant to be made with chicken, but the chicken can technically be replaced by tofu to make the dish vegitarian.

This recipe is NOTcomplete

Ingredients

  • 1/2t Salt
  • 2T Light soy sauce
  • 1T Shaoxing Wine
  • 1T Corn Starch
  • 600g Chicken
  • 6 cloves of garlic and an equal amount of ginger
  • 8 dried red chili peppers
  • 2 bunches of scallions (8-10)
  • 2t dark soy sauce

Steps

  1. Mix salt, light soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, corn starch for marinade.
  2. Cut chicken into 1cm strips, then into small cubes, then marinate.
  3. Peel and slice ginger and garlic thinly
  4. remove seeds of chili and cut into 1cm pieces.
  5. Cut the scallions into 1cm lengths, use only white parts.
    6. 2t dark soy auce, 2t light soy sauce, 2T Chinkiang vinegar, 1T sesame oil, 1T water, 2T sugar, 2t corn starch: mix for sauce. Heat 3T peanut oil in wok until not quite smoking. Add chilis and 1/2 T Sichuan pepper for just a few seconds (don't burn). Add marinated chicken and stirfry until cubes have separated. Add ginger, garlic and scallions; stirfry until meak just cooked. Stir the sauce and add it; as soon as it thickens add 200g peanuts and serve.
